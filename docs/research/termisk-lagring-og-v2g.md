## Thermal/sand storage and V2G/V2H: honest maturity note

### 1. Thermal and sand storage

Polar Night Energy's "sand battery" stores electricity as high-temperature heat in granular solids (sand or crushed soapstone). Resistive heaters warm air that circulates through the storage medium; the stored heat is later released through a heat exchanger for district heating or industrial process heat (roughly 60-400 C output).

Two real installations exist. The world's first commercial unit in Kankaanpaeae (utility Vatajankoski) has run since July 2022: 200 kW thermal power, 8 MWh capacity, 100 tonnes of sand, core around 500 C, with a stated overall efficiency of about 60-75% at this 200 kW scale (Polar Night Energy projects around 85% only at the larger ~2 MW scale, and around 90% at ~10 MW). The world's largest, in Pornainen (Loviisan Lampo), was commissioned in June 2025: 1 MW, 100 MWh, around 2,000 tonnes of crushed soapstone, roughly 13 m tall by 15 m wide. It covers close to a month of summer heat demand and about a week in winter, eliminating oil and cutting woodchip combustion by around 60%.

Two honesty points. First, the quoted 60-90% efficiencies are for heat output, not electricity; heat-to-electricity conversion is under development and not operational. Second, both units feed utility district-heating networks. A shared community energy base for one coastal development is a concept, not a delivered product: seasonal thermal storage (for example Danish pit stores such as Vojens, 200,000 m3, supplying about half a network's annual heat) is commercially proven at district scale, but a specific site application requires a dedicated engineering and feasibility study.

### 2. V2G / V2H maturity

Vehicle-to-grid and vehicle-to-home are emerging, not mainstream. The enabling standard is ISO 15118-20:2022 ("Road vehicles - Vehicle to grid communication interface - Part 20"), which defines bidirectional power transfer between vehicle and charger, with TLS-secured, certificate-based communication. Both sides must support it: the vehicle needs a bidirectional-capable battery management system, and the charger needs bidirectional power electronics plus ISO 15118-20 communication.

In Europe, France is currently the clearest consumer market (Renault 5 with a bidirectional AC charger since 2024), while Germany, the Netherlands and the UK remain largely in pilots. Norway demonstrated V2G in 2020 (Campus Evenstad, a 9.5 kW Nissan Leaf feed-in) but has no documented mainstream consumer product as of June 2026. Regulatory mandate dates (AFIR, ISO 15118 adoption) circulate in industry guides but were not confirmed against primary EU texts; Norway is EEA, so applicability differs. Treat all V2G/V2H claims as forward-looking and requiring professional verification.
