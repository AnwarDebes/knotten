# Building rules and Sorlandet vernacular for the indicative 3D homes

For the immersive experience (SPEC-27 and later), the homes are indicative
massing, not an approved design (the project is pre-zoning). This note records
the real Norwegian rules and the regional vernacular that the indicative massing
is generated from, so it stays honest and plausible. Compiled 2026-06-27.

Project convention: re-verify every figure against the cited primary source
before it is published or relied on. Confidence reflects source quality, not a
substitute for that re-check.

## Heights, storeys and setbacks

- Default height ceiling when no plan authorises more, pbl section 29-4 first
  paragraph: gesimshoyde (eave) max 8 m and monehoyde (ridge) max 9 m without a
  plan under chapter 11 or 12. Confidence: high. Source: Lovdata,
  https://lovdata.no/lov/2008-06-27-71/§29-4 . Caveat: these are the limits above
  which a plan is needed, not an automatic right; the municipality still approves
  placement and a plan can set lower limits.
- How height is measured, TEK17 section 6-2: from the average finished terrain
  around the building. On a slope the downhill, sea-facing facade reads taller.
  Represent from average surrounding terrain. Source: dibk.no,
  https://www.dibk.no/regelverk/byggteknisk-forskrift-tek17/6/6-2 .
- Minimum clear room height, TEK17 section 12-7: 2.4 m for rooms for lasting
  stay, 2.2 m otherwise. Typical floor-to-floor is about 2.7 to 3.0 m
  (synthesised, not a single statutory figure, flag as an assumption). A
  2-storey home then lands around 5.4 to 6.0 m to the eave, under the 8 m limit.
- Setback to a neighbour boundary, pbl section 29-4 second paragraph: at least
  4 m and at least half the building height. Small detached outbuildings up to
  50 m2 may sit 1.0 m from the boundary. Road setback (veglova section 29
  defaults): 50 m from riks/fylkesveg, 15 m from kommunal veg, but a plan
  commonly sets a much smaller line. Confidence: high.
- Utilisation (BYA / percent-BYA): TEK17 sets no default; it is fixed per area by
  the plan. Detached/small-house residential is commonly about 20 to 30 percent
  (a real-world generalisation, not statutory; parking counts toward BYA). Treat
  as an assumption.

## The 100-metre sea belt (critical near the fjord)

- pbl section 1-8: in the 100-metre belt along the sea, measured horizontally
  from the shoreline at ordinary high water, no measures other than facade
  changes may be carried out, unless a plan-fixed byggegrense or a listed
  exception applies. The Agder coast falls in the stricter management zone
  (FOR-2021-05-28-1666). Confidence: high. Source: Lovdata,
  https://lovdata.no/lov/2008-06-27-71/§1-8 .
- For the simulation: keep indicative homes landward of the 100 m line unless a
  future plan establishes a closer byggegrense; never imply an automatic right
  to build inside the belt. Sniksfjorden is a sea arm, so the belt applies.

## Sorlandet / Agder coastal vernacular (the Sorlandshus)

Primary local source: "Veileder for bevaringsomradene", Mandal kommune 2007
(Mandal is now part of Lindesnes), hosted by dibk.no:
https://www.dibk.no/globalassets/byggeskikk/lokale-byggeskikkveiledere/mandal---veileder-for-bevaringsomradene.pdf
. Plus Store norske leksikon "sorlandshus", https://snl.no/sorlandshus .

- Storeys: typically 1 or 2 (the classic small Mandal house is 1 storey; larger
  classic houses 1.5 to 2 on a high masonry base). Confidence: high.
- Roof: gable (saltak), pitch 38 to 45 degrees in Mandal (broader band 30 to 45
  across the region), modest eave and gable overhangs on older houses. Red or
  black clay tile (vingetegl). Confidence: high.
- Cladding: painted timber boarding, both horizontal (liggende) weatherboard and
  vertical (staaende) tommermannskledning occur; horizontal is at least as
  typical west of Kristiansand. Colour: white is dominant on the coast (with
  ochre/mone-red a minority accent). White-painted timber is the safe default.
- Windows: small-paned, vertically proportioned, in a regular rhythm; avoid wide
  modern horizontal glazing for an honest vernacular read.
- Base: high stone/masonry grunnmur.

## Zoning status for the site

No reguleringsplan named Snik, Rodberg or Sniksfjorden was found via web search
(arealplaner.no for Lindesnes, kommune 4205, is a JavaScript app that could not
be searched programmatically, so treat "none found" as not-certified, not a
confirmed negative). The governing higher-level plan for an unregulated parcel is
Lindesnes kommuneplanens arealdel 2019 to 2030. The absence of a site plan
supports the pre-zoning, indicative framing. Verify in the municipal plan
register and Kartverket "Se eiendom" before stating anything as fact.
