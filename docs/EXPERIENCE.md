# The Knotten 3D experience: how to show it

A walkable 3D of the Knotten site at Rodberg, built so an investor who cannot
travel there can still understand the place. The terrain, sun, distances and
energy figures are real; the buildings are indicative until the zoning and design
are settled. This note is the plain-language guide to opening it and showing it.

## Opening it

- From the home page or the area page, click the big "Walk Knotten" link, or go
  straight to `/opplev` (Norwegian) or `/experience` (English).
- The world enters on its own, no clicking required. A short branded loader runs
  while the real terrain from Kartverket streams in.
- It needs a computer with a normal browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox).
  Phones and machines without 3D support get a still image and the same facts in
  text, plus the interest form, so no one hits a broken page.
- Tip: click into the page (or keep the tab in front) so it runs at full speed.
  In a background tab the browser slows animation down to save power.

## The controls

- **Walk**: W A S D or the arrow keys. The investor turns to face the way he
  walks.
- **Look**: drag with the mouse, or click once to capture the mouse and look
  freely. It spins a full 360 degrees smoothly, and tilts from the sky down to
  almost straight down.
- **Faster**: hold Shift to sprint, or pick 2x or 3x on the Speed control.
- **Rise and look from above**: Space to go up, C to come back down, for an
  aerial overview of the whole site.
- **Time and season**: the Summer/Winter and Morning/Midday/Evening buttons move
  the real sun for 58 degrees north, so you can show the long summer light and
  the low winter sun.
- The small map in the corner shows the plots and where you are.

## A five-minute walkthrough for an investor

1. Open the link and let it load; read the one-line framing as it appears.
2. Walk down toward the fjord to show the sea views and the real slope.
3. Step into the show-home and look out through the sea-facing window.
4. Press Space to rise and look down over the whole site and the coastline.
5. Switch to Winter and Evening to show the light through the year.
6. Point out the energy flowing between the homes and the rooftop solar, and read
   the real figure in the corner (about 1010 kWh/kWp at 58 degrees north).
7. Close with the "Register interest" button.

## What is real, and what is indicative

- **Real**: the landform (Kartverket elevation data), the sun and daylight for the
  exact location, the sea, the distances and the true directions to Vigeland,
  Mandal, Kristiansand and Lindesnes lighthouse, and the energy figures.
- **Indicative**: the buildings, their size, height, count and placement, and the
  show-home interior. They are larger multi-storey blocks to read as a real
  development, which assumes a denser zoning plan; nothing is an approved design.
  A persistent "indicative massing" badge and the text below the world say so.
- The 100-metre building-free belt along the water (pbl 1-8) is respected.
