# ADR-0009: MapLibre GL with OpenStreetMap for the neighbourhood map

## Status

Accepted, 2026-06-18.

## Context

The site needs a map to communicate the neighbourhood at Rødberg, Sniksfjorden (Lindesnes kommune, Agder), at the outlet of the Audna river: walking and driving distances, and nearby amenities. The map is informational, not transactional. There is no routing-as-a-service requirement and no need for live traffic, Street View, or place reviews.

Two constraints shape the choice:

- Privacy. The rest of the stack is built to avoid tracking: Plausible (EU, cookieless) for analytics, no cookie banner, email and database in the EU/EEA only. A map widget that loads third-party trackers or sets advertising cookies would undermine this and would force a consent banner that the project otherwise does not need.
- Licence cleanliness. Data sources on the project are already vetted per licence: Kartverket Høydedata terrain is open (CC BY 4.0, commercial use permitted), Norge i bilder orthophotos are not open (Geovekst-licensed, so excluded from public use), and OpenStreetMap is ODbL. The map layer must be one whose terms are clear and whose attribution can be honoured in full.

The map is one component among data-driven placeholders. Unit and plot counts are unknown, and no real site photography exists yet, so the map should not imply precision the project does not have. A schematic, attribution-correct map fits that posture better than a polished proprietary product that suggests finality.

## Decision

Use MapLibre GL with OpenStreetMap data for the neighbourhood and amenities map.

- Rendering: MapLibre GL (open source, BSD-licensed), self-hosted as a static asset. No SDK that phones home, no account-bound API key embedded in the page.
- Data: OpenStreetMap, under ODbL. Attribution "© OpenStreetMap contributors" is shown on the map in line with the ODbL and the OSM attribution guidelines.
- Tiles: a tile source whose terms explicitly permit the expected traffic. The specific provider (whether a hosted OSM-based tile service on a paid or free-tier plan, or self-rendered tiles) is documented in the deployment configuration together with its usage limits, so the choice can be audited and re-checked as traffic grows. The default OSM Foundation tile servers are not used in production, since their tile usage policy does not cover application or commercial traffic.
- Tracking: none. No tracking cookies, no third-party analytics or advertising scripts loaded by the map. Map interaction events, if measured at all, go through the existing cookieless Plausible setup.

## Alternatives considered

- Google Maps. Mature, familiar, strong place and routing data. Rejected: it is proprietary, it loads third-party tracking and typically sets cookies (which would trigger a consent banner the project is built to avoid), it ties the project to per-load billing and Google account terms, and its data cannot be reused under an open licence. The privacy cost alone is disqualifying for this stack.
- Mapbox. High-quality rendering and a good developer experience, and Mapbox GL itself was the ancestor of MapLibre. Rejected: the current Mapbox GL JS is under proprietary terms, usage is account-bound and metered, and the hosted product carries telemetry by default. MapLibre is the open fork that gives the same rendering model without the proprietary licence, the mandatory account, or the telemetry.
- Self-rendered raster tiles with a simpler viewer (for example a Leaflet plus raster setup). A reasonable fallback and lower in operational complexity, but it gives up vector rendering, smooth zoom, and cheap restyling. Kept on the table as a degradation path if vector tile hosting proves disproportionate, but not the primary choice.

Common thread: the proprietary options (Google, Mapbox) bring trackers, cost, and licence lock-in that conflict with the privacy and licence-cleanliness goals. MapLibre plus OSM meets the goals directly.

## Consequences

- Attribution "© OpenStreetMap contributors" is rendered on the map at all times, satisfying the ODbL. If any ODbL-derived data is processed and redistributed, the share-alike obligation is reviewed at that point; for plain display of tiles this does not arise.
- The chosen tile provider and its usage terms (rate limits, monthly tile or request ceilings, commercial-use clause) are documented in the deployment configuration. This is a live obligation: if traffic approaches the provider's limits, the provider or plan is revisited, and self-hosting tiles remains the escape hatch.
- No tracking cookies are set by the map, and no consent banner is required on the map's account. This keeps the cookieless, banner-free posture intact across the whole site.
- Trade-off accepted: place metadata (business names, opening hours, reviews) is sparser and less uniformly maintained than Google's. For an informational distances-and-amenities map this is acceptable; where a specific amenity matters, it can be placed manually rather than relying on third-party place data.
- Trade-off accepted: vector tile hosting and styling add some operational and build-time work compared with dropping in a hosted proprietary widget. This is the cost of owning the privacy and licence properties, and it is bounded because MapLibre is self-hosted and the tile source is the only external dependency.
- Self-hosting MapLibre means its version is maintained in-project (security patches, breaking changes in style spec). This is a normal dependency-maintenance cost and is preferred over runtime dependence on a vendor SDK.
- The map's neutrality matches the data-driven placeholder approach used elsewhere: it shows the real geography of Rødberg and Sniksfjorden without implying decisions (unit counts, layouts) that are not yet made.
